Do We Have Free Will?

Jan 6th 2025 4 min read

Do We Have Free Will?

Are we truly in control of our choices, or are we just following an intricate script written by the universe?

The Illusion of Choice

Every day, we make choices: what to eat, where to go, what career to pursue, whom to love. We believe we are making these choices freely. But how much of this freedom is real? If every decision we make is shaped by our upbringing, genetics, past experiences, and subconscious biases, then where exactly does our "free" will begin?

"Man can do what he wills, but he cannot will what he wills."

Imagine rewinding your life to the moment before you made a big decision. If you played that moment again under the exact same conditions, would you choose differently? If not, then was it really a choice at all?

The Deterministic Universe

Science tells us that the universe operates under fixed physical laws. Every action has a cause, and every cause has a preceding cause. This leads to a troubling idea: if the past dictates the present, and the present dictates the future, then everything—including our thoughts and choices—is already set in motion.

If determinism is true, then free will is an illusion. You might feel like you freely decided to read this article, but in reality, every event in your past—your education, your interests, even the neurons firing in your brain—inevitably led you here.

The Role of Biology

Neuroscience adds another layer to this debate. Experiments have shown that our brains make decisions before we are consciously aware of them. In other words, by the time we think we're making a choice, our brains have already made it for us.

So what does this mean? Are we just machines running biological programs, fooling ourselves into thinking we are in control?

Can We Still Find Meaning?

If free will is an illusion, does it make life meaningless? Not necessarily. Even if our paths are predetermined, we still experience joy, pain, love, and curiosity. These emotions feel real to us, and maybe that's enough.

Perhaps meaning isn't found in the ability to choose, but in how we experience the journey—even if it's one we were always meant to take.

The Unresolved Question

Maybe we do have free will, or maybe we don't. Maybe our minds exist beyond physics in some unknown way, or maybe we are just passengers in a cosmic flow we cannot control.

In the end, whether we are free or bound by fate, one thing remains the same: we must live, act, and find purpose in the choices—real or illusory—that define our lives.

Are we the authors of our own stories, or are we just reading lines from a script written long before we were born?
